SECOND REVIEW CONFERENCE OF THE APLC/CONF/2009/6 STATES PARTIES TO THE CONVENTION 23 November 2009 ON THE PROHIBITION OF THE USE, STOCKPILING, PRODUCTION AND TRANSFER OF ANTI-PERSONNEL MINES AND ON THEIR DESTRUCTION Original: ENGLISH Cartagena de Indias, 30 November-4 December 2009 Item 5 of the provisional agenda Adoption of the budget ESTIMATED COSTS FOR CONVENING THE TENTH MEETING OF THE STATES PARTIES TO THE CONVENTION ON THE PROHIBITION OF THE USE, STOCKPILING, PRODUCTION AND TRANSFER OF ANTI-PERSONNEL MINES AND ON THEIR DESTRUCTION Note by the Secretariat The Second Review Conference of the States Parties to the Convention on the Prohibition of the Use, Stockpiling, Production and Transfer of Anti-Personnel Mines and on Their Destruction, held in Cartagena de Indias, Colombia, from 30 November to 4 December 2009, decided to convene the Tenth Meeting of the States Parties to the Convention in Geneva, Switzerland, from 29 November to 3 December 2010. 2. This document is submitted pursuant to the above-mentioned request and provides estimated costs in the amount of US$472,000 for conference and non-conference servicing of the Tenth Meeting of the States Parties. A breakdown of these costs is provided in the attached table. It should be noted that the costs are estimated on the basis of past experience and anticipated workload. The actual costs will be determined after the closing of the Meeting when the exact workload is known. At that time, any adjustments in contributions by the participants sharing the costs will be made accordingly. 3. With regard to the financial arrangements, it will be recalled that, in accordance with article 14 (1) of the Convention, "the costs... shall be borne by the States Parties and States not parties to this Convention participating therein, in accordance with the United Nations scale of assessment adjusted appropriately". 4. Since preparations for the Tenth Meeting of the States Parties will involve costs that will need to be defrayed beforehand, and as the holding the Tenth Meeting shall have no financial implications for the regular budget of the United Nations, the Secretariat will prepare and issue assessment notices as soon as the as the Second Review Conference of the States Parties has accepted the attached estimated costs in accordance with the established practice. 5. The States Parties should proceed with the payment of their assessed share of the estimated costs as soon as the assessments notices have been received.
