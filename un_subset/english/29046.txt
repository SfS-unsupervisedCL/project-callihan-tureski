Summary statement by the Secretary-General on matters of which the Security Council is seized and on the stage reached in their consideration Addendum Pursuant to rule 11 of the provisional rules of procedure of the Security Council, the Secretary-General is submitting the following summary statement. The list of items of which the Security Council is seized is contained in documents S/2004/20 of 14 February 2004 and S/2004/20/Add.4 of 5 March 2004. During the week ending 6 March 2004, the Security Council took action on the following items: The question concerning Haiti (see S/25070/Add.24 and Corr.1, 34, 35, 37, 38, 41, 43 and 46; S/1994/20/Add.1, 11, 17, 25, 27, 30, 38, 40 and 47; S/1995/40/Add.4, 16, 30 and 45; S/1996/15/Add.8, 25, 47 and 48; S/1997/40/Add.30 and 47; S/1998/44/Add.12 and 47; S/1999/25/Add.47; S/2000/40/Add.10; and S/2004/20/Add.8; see also S/22110/Add.39) The Security Council resumed its consideration of the item at its 4919th meeting, held on 29 February 2004 in accordance with the understanding reached in its prior consultations. The President, with the consent of the Council, invited the representative of Haiti, at his request, to participate in the discussion without the right to vote. The President drew attention to a draft resolution (S/2004/164) that had been prepared in the course of the Council's prior consultations. The Security Council proceeded to vote on draft resolution S/2004/164, and adopted it unanimously as resolution 1529 (2004) (for the text, see S/RES/1529 (2004); to be issued in Official Records of the Security Council, Resolutions and Decisions of the Security Council, 1 August 2003-31 July 2004). The situation in Bosnia and Herzegovina (see S/23370/Add.36, 40, 43 and 45; S/25070/Add.1, 4, 7-9, 11-13, 15, 16, 18, 19, 22, 23, 24 and Corr.1, 26, 29, 34, 37 and 45; S/1994/20 and Add.4, 6, 8, 10, 13-17, 20, 21, 23, 25, 34, 37, 38, 44-47 and 49; S/1995/40 and Add.1, 6, 14, 15, 17, 18, 24, 26-29, 31, 35-37, 40 and 47-50; S/1996/15/Add.13, 31, 40 and 49; S/1997/40/Add.6, 10, 12, 19, 23 and 50; S/1998/44/Add.11, 20, 24 and 28; S/1999/25/Add.23, 30, 42, 44 and 45; S/2000/40/Add.11, 18, 23, 24, 27, 32, 42, 45 and 49; S/2001/15/Add.12, 24, 25, 38 and 49; S/2002/30/Add.9, 24, 26, 27, 42 and 49; and S/2003/40/Add.27 and 40; see also S/22110/Add.38, 47 and 50; S/23370/Add.1, 5, 7, 14, 16, 19, 21, 23, 24, 26, 28, 29, 31, 32, 35, 37, 40, 46, 49 and 50; S/25070/Add.4, 8, 13, 17, 19, 21, 24 and Corr.1, 26, 28, 30, 32, 33, 37 and 39-42; S/1994/20/Add.12, 26, 31, 45 and 49; S/1995/40/Add.2, 5, 12, 16, 18, 19, 23, 30, 32, 39, 44, 46, 47 and 50; S/1996/15/Add.1, 2, 4, 6-8, 18, 20, 21, 26, 28, 30, 32, 37, 39, 45, 47 and 50; S/1997/40/Add.2, 4, 9, 11, 14, 16, 18, 21, 28, 34, 37, 42, 47, 48 and 50; S/1998/44/Add.2, 6, 9, 19, 26, 29, 34, 44 and 46; S/1999/25/Add.1-3, 7, 11, 17, 18, 22, 27, 31, 43 and 51; S/2000/40/Add.1, 8, 21, 24, 27, 46 and 47; S/2001/15/Add.2, 3, 6, 17, 28 and 48; S/2002/30/Add.2, 19, 23, 27, 29, 40, 43, 49 and 50; and S/2003/40/Add.20, 31, 34, 35 and 40) The Security Council resumed its consideration of the item at its 4920th meeting, held on 3 March 2004 in accordance with the understanding reached in its prior consultations, having before it the letter dated 19 February 2004 from the Secretary-General addressed to the President of the Security Council (S/2004/126). Threats to international peace and security caused by terrorist acts (see S/2001/15/Add.37, 39 and 46; S/2002/30/Add.2, 15, 25, 39-42, 49 and 50; S/2003/40/Add.2, 6, 7, 13, 18, 29, 30, 33, 41 and 46; and S/2004/40/Add.2 and 4; see also S/1998/44/Add.32; and S/2003/40/Add.3) The Security Council resumed its consideration of the item at its 4921st meeting, held on 4 March 2004 in accordance with the understanding reached in its prior consultations, having before it the letter dated 19 February 2004 from the Chairman of the Security Council Committee established pursuant to resolution 1373 (2001) concerning counter-terrorism addressed to the President of the Security Council (S/2004/124).
