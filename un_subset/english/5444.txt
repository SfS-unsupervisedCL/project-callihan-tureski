UNITED NATIONS Distr. GENERAL S/1998/44/Add.43 6 November 1998 ORIGINAL: ENGLISH SUMMARY STATEMENT BY THE SECRETARY-GENERAL ON MATTERS OF WHICH THE SECURITY COUNCIL IS SEIZED AND ON THE STAGE REACHED IN THEIR CONSIDERATION Addendum Pursuant to rule 11 of the provisional rules of procedure of the Security Council, the Secretary-General is submitting the following summary statement. The list of items of which the Security Council is seized is contained in documents S/1998/44 of 9 January 1998, S/1998/44/Add.13 of 9 April 1998, S/1998/44/Add.16 of 1 May 1998, S/1998/44/Add.19 of 22 May 1998, S/1998/44/Add.25 of 2 July 1998, S/1998/44/Add.26 of 10 July 1998, S/1998/44/Add.28 of 24 July 1998, and S/1998/44/Add.32 of 21 August 1998. During the week ending 31 October 1998, the Security Council took action on the following item: The situation concerning Western Sahara (see S/11593/Add.42 and 44; S/19420/Add.38; S/21100/Add.25; S/22110/Add.17; S/23370; S/25070/Add.9; S/1994/20/Add.12, 29 and 45; S/1995/40/Add.1, 14, 20, 25, 37 and 50; S/1996/15/Add.21 and 47; S/1997/40/Add.11, 20, 39 and 42; and S/1998/44/Add.4, 15 and 29) The Security Council resumed its consideration of the item at its 3938th meeting, on 30 October 1998, in accordance with the understanding reached in its prior consultations, having before it the report of the Secretary-General on the situation concerning Western Sahara (S/1998/997). The President drew attention to the text of a draft resolution (S/1998/1011) which had been prepared in the course of the Council's prior consultations. The Security Council proceeded to vote on draft resolution S/1998/1011, and adopted it unanimously as resolution 1204 (1998) (for the text, see S/RES/1204 (1998); to be issued in Official Records of the Security Council, Fifty-third Year, Resolutions and Decisions of the Security Council, 1998).
