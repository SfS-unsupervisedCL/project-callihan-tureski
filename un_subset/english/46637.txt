Resumed organizational session for 2008 29 and 30 April 2008 Agenda item 4 Elections, nominations, confirmations and appointments Election of 11 members of the Executive Board of the United Nations Development Programme/United Nations Population Fund Note by the Secretary-General Addendum 1. The Executive Board of the United Nations Development Programme/United Nations Population Fund has 36 members, elected with due regard to equitable geographical representation and other relevant factors, with a view to ensuring the most effective and broadest representation. 2. In accordance with General Assembly resolution 48/162, the Economic and Social Council is to elect, at its resumed organizational session for 2008, 11 members of the Executive Board from among States Members of the United Nations or members of the specialized agencies or of the International Atomic Energy Agency, for a three-year term beginning on 1 January 2009, to fill the vacancies that will occur on 31 December 2008 upon the expiration of the terms of the following members: Algeria, Bangladesh, Benin, Ecuador, Iceland, Jamaica, Japan, Norway, Pakistan, Russian Federation and Switzerland. 3. The 11 members are to be elected according to the following pattern: Two members from African States; Two members from Asian States; One member from Eastern European States; Two members from Latin American and Caribbean States; Four members from Western European and other States. 4. The membership of the Executive Board in 2008 is listed in the annex to the present note.
